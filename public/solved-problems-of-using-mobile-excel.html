Filling Cells on Mobile

Filling cells in Excel is a common task, whether you're inputting a series of numbers, dates, or text. Here's how you can efficiently fill cells on your mobile device:


  • Select the Initial Cell: Tap on the initial cell from where you want to begin filling.Then tap Fill.
  • Drag the Fill Handle: Drag the fill handle (a small square located at the bottom right corner of the selected cell) in the desired direction. Excel will automatically populate the subsequent cells with a series of values based on the pattern detected.

    • Using the Format Painter

    The Format Painter feature in Excel is incredibly useful for applying formatting styles to different cells, saving you time and effort. Here's how you can use the Format Painter on your mobile device:

  • Select the Source Cell: Tap and the cell with the desired formatting that you want to copy.
  • Tap "fill Color": choose the color
  • Tap fill then drag the range to the cell you want to format the color

    • Removing Duplicates

    When working with large datasets, it's essential to identify and remove duplicate entries. Here's how you can do it on your mobile device:

  • Tap on an empty cell.

  • On the function tab type =UNIQUE
  • Select the Data Range: Tap and drag to select the range of cells or columns containing the data where you want to remove duplicates.

  • The new data will be free of duplicate entries
