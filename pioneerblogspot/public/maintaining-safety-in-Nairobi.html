Maintaining Safety in Nairobi

Nairobi, a bustling metropolis filled with vibrant energy, offers a unique blend of cultures and experiences. However, like any major city, it's important to prioritize safety, especially when it comes to your valuable items. In this guide, we'll explore practical tips to maintain safety while navigating Nairobi.

Protecting Your Valuables:

  1. Conceal Your Electronics:
    • Your phone and other electronic devices are among your most valuable possessions. Avoid using them openly in crowded spaces or when you're alone.
    • Keep your phone out of sight by not carrying it openly in your hands. Conceal it in a less obvious manner, such as placing it in the center of a book if you're a student.
    • Set your phone to vibrate to avoid drawing attention with loud ringtones. Hold it securely, as thieves on motorbikes may target loosely gripped phones.
  2. Stay Alert and Purposeful:
    • Walk with a purpose and confidence. Avoid appearing lost, as this can attract unwanted attention.
    • If you need directions, approach police officers or security officers in reputable establishments like banks. They are reliable sources for assistance.
  3. Inform Others of Your Whereabouts:
    • Never meet people in private areas without informing someone about your location.
    • Keep a record of your email and consider adding a recovery email or setting up two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.
  4. Road Safety Awareness:
    • Be cautious when crossing roads, as motorbikes and cars may pose a threat. Stay situationally aware to prevent accidents, especially with vehicles maneuvering through traffic.
  5. Immediate Action After Theft:
    • If your phone is stolen, act promptly. Visit the nearest Safaricom shop to close your SIM card, preventing fraudulent activities.
    • Report the incident to the authorities and take necessary steps to track your device using features like "Find My Device."
  6. Hygiene Matters:
    • Be selective with food vendors and prioritize those who handle food hygienically. Wash anything washable to maintain cleanliness and reduce health risks.
